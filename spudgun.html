Spud Cannon Optimization for Rosco (3-inch Reservoir)

Overview

This spud cannon is optimized for maximum distance (~60–75 meters) to deploy a ~0.227 kg PETG projectile (170 g sinker, hook for 30–40 inch striped bass, 16–33 cm³ bait) for striped bass fishing, using a 38.1 mm ABS barrel and 25.4 mm sprinkler valve with a 3-inch reservoir.

Optimized Configuration

Reservoir: 76.2 mm (3-inch) ABS pipe, 508 mm length

  • Volume: ~4.0 liters (4000 cm³)
  • Why: Balances air volume and portability for efficient energy transfer

Barrel: 38.1 mm (1.5-inch) ABS pipe, 1219 mm length

  • Volume: ~1353 cm³
  • Why: Maximizes acceleration for ~45–52 m/s muzzle velocity

Air Pressure: 120 psi

  • Why: Boosts velocity safely within Schedule 40 ABS limits (~260 psi rating)

Performance

  • Muzzle Velocity: ~45–52 m/s
  • Range: ~60–75 meters at 40° launch angle (with drag and mid-air projectile split)
  • Weight: ~2.0–2.3 kg (reservoir ~1.3 kg, barrel ~0.5 kg, plus fittings)

Recommendations

  • Build: Construct with 508 mm 3-inch reservoir, 1219 mm barrel, operate at 120 psi.
  • Test: Launch at 35°, 40°, 45°; measure bait landing distance. Use chronograph for velocity if available.
  • Safety: Hydrostatically pressure-test at 180 psi. Consider PVC barrel for durability.
  • Tweak: If range <60 m, try 25.4–31.8 mm valve or 1270 mm barrel; if too heavy, shorten reservoir to 457 mm (~3.6 liters).

Notes for Rosco

Ensure the PETG projectile splits reliably 1–2 seconds into flight (~15–30 m downrange). Use 20–30 lb braided fishing line to minimize drag. The 3-inch x 508 mm reservoir matches the 4-inch x 381 mm performance but is slightly heavier (~2.3 kg vs. 2.0 kg total). Share test results for further optimization!
