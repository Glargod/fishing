Key Points

  • The 21-year-old (21 yo), a co-owner of the vehicle, was charged with stunting (170 km/h in a 110 km/h zone) and driving without insurance (not listed on the policy), holding a Class 7 (Learner's) license.
  • The parent, as the registered owner, faces impoundment fees and potential vehicle registration suspension.
  • The 21 yo faces a $2,400 stunting fine, $1,250 no-insurance fine, 6-month license suspension, and 6 demerit points.
  • Both may see insurance premium increases; the 21 yo will face high-risk insurance costs for 3–6 years.
  • Co-ownership complicates insurance but not legal penalties.

Penalties for the Parent

As the registered owner, the parent’s liabilities stem from the vehicle’s registration and insurance status. The 21 yo’s co-ownership does not shift these responsibilities:

  • Impoundment Fees: The parent must pay towing and storage fees for the 7-day impoundment due to the stunting charge, typically $300–$500.
  • Vehicle Registration Suspension: Under Section 230(1) and Section 205 of the Motor Vehicle Act, driving without insurance (21 yo not listed on the policy) may lead to suspension of the vehicle’s registration until proof of insurance for all drivers is provided.
  • Insurance Premium Increases: The parent’s insurance premiums may rise due to an unlisted household driver (the 21 yo, a co-owner) operating the vehicle. Insurers may view this as a policy breach, potentially leading to higher rates or cancellation risk.
  • No Criminal Liability: The parent faces no fines or license suspension, as they weren’t driving. Penalties are administrative or financial.

Penalties for the 21-Year-Old

As co-owner and driver with a Class 7 license, the 21 yo faces penalties for stunting and driving without insurance:

  • Stunting Penalties:
    • Fine: $2,400 for driving 170 km/h in a 110 km/h zone.
    • Demerit Points: 6 points, triggering a 6-month license suspension.
    • GDL Impact: Resets 12-month Class 7 period (8 months with driver training), delaying Class 5N. Post-suspension, pays ~$115 reinstatement fee and may retake tests.
  • Driving Without Insurance:
    • Fine: $1,250 ($1,000 penalty, $100 court costs, $150 victim surcharge) for not being listed on the policy.
  • Insurance Impact: High-risk status, with premiums potentially $5,000–$8,000/year for 3–6 years. High-risk insurers may require upfront payment.
  • Co-Ownership: Being on the title doesn’t increase legal penalties but complicates insurance, as co-owners in the household must be listed on the policy.

Future Prospects

  • Short-Term (1–2 Years):
    • 21 yo: No driving for 6 months, disrupting work/school. Fines ($3,650 total), impound fees (if shared), and reinstatement costs (~$115) strain finances. Living with parents eases transport but may cause tension.
    • Parent: Pays $300–$500 impound fees and faces potential registration suspension, requiring insurance updates. Premium increases add pressure.
  • Long-Term (3–5 Years):
    • 21 yo: High insurance costs persist for 3–6 years. A clean record allows GDL progression (Class 5N, then Class 5 in ~2–3 years). Driving-related jobs may be affected.
    • Parent: Insurance stabilizes if 21 yo is added to the policy and no further incidents occur. Registration issues resolve with compliance.
  • Family Dynamics: Co-ownership and registration suggest shared responsibility, possibly straining relations. The parent’s Indigenous status may offer access to legal aid or community support.

Recommendations

  • Legal: Consult a lawyer to explore reducing charges (stunting or no insurance), though difficult. Paying fines accepts convictions.
  • Insurance: Both should update the policy to list the 21 yo as a driver to avoid future violations. The 21 yo should compare high-risk insurer quotes.
  • Financial: Budget for fines ($3,650 for 21 yo, $300–$500 for parent) and fees to avoid penalties like jail (rare for first offenses).
  • Driving: Post-suspension, the 21 yo must adhere to Class 7 rules (supervision, zero BAC, curfew). Driver training could shorten GDL and improve insurance prospects.
