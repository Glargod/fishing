1. Concept Overview

The magneto-thermovoltaic (MTV) device converts conductive heat into electrical current via thermoelectrics, cooling the source through heat extraction. Neodymium magnets (0.5-1.5 T) act as a “traffic police,” directing electron flow or building voltage like a capacitor, boosting output by ~10-20% without directly affecting efficiency. It targets xAI’s GPU racks, Nova Scotia trailers, and hybrid EVs, with a long-term 90% efficiency goal.

Key Features:

  • Converts conductive heat (50-900°C) to electricity, cooling the source.
  • Uses hard drive magnets to enhance current/voltage, reducing power bills.
  • Targets 10-15% efficiency near-term, 90% long-term with material breakthroughs.
  • Applications: xAI data centers, off-grid trailers, hybrid EVs.

2. Technical Mechanism

2.1 Magneto-Thermoelectric Core

Principle: A thermoelectric generator (TEG, e.g., Bi₂Te₃) converts a temperature gradient (50-900°C) into voltage via the Seebeck effect. Neodymium magnets (0.5-1.5 T, from hard drives) apply a Lorentz force to direct electron flow, reducing resistance losses, or induce a Hall-like voltage, boosting output by ~10-20%. Heat extraction cools the source, with a feedback-controlled heat sink optimizing the gradient.

Equations:

  • Seebeck voltage: V = S × ΔT, where S is the Seebeck coefficient, ΔT is the temperature difference.
  • Power output: P = V × I, where I is current enhanced by magnetic field.
  • Current enhancement: ΔI/I ≈ μB, where μ is carrier mobility, B is magnetic field (0.5-1.5 T).
  • Hall-like voltage: V_H = I × B / (n × q × t), where n is carrier density, q is charge, t is thickness.
  • Cooling rate: ΔT = q_out / (m × c), where q_out is heat extracted, m is source mass, c is specific heat.

Efficiency: Baseline TEG efficiency ~5-10% at 50-300°C (ZT ~1-1.5). Magnets boost output by ~10-20% (e.g., 0.1 A to 0.11-0.12 A, 0.1-0.5 V added). Near-term target: 10-15% with high-ZT materials (e.g., MgAgSb). Long-term 90% requires revolutionary materials (ZT > 10). Cooling: ~20-50°C for a 1 kg source.

6. Example Applications

6.1 Trailer Campfire-Powered Appliance

Scenario: A campfire (500°C) with a ceramic dome (0.01 m² TEG) powers a 5 W LED, cooling the enclosure.

Calculations:

  • Heat Input: 5000 W total, dome captures 80% = 4000 W (0.01 m² = 40 W).
  • Output: 10% efficiency = 4 W, +10-20% with magnets = 4.4-4.8 W (powers LED).
  • Cooling: Extracts ~3.2 W, cooling dome by ~50°C (10 kg, c ~900 J/kg·K).
  • Savings: ~$100/year/unit (off-grid, $0.30/kWh).
