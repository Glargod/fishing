Technical Review: Geothermal Potential in Springhill, Nova Scotia's Flooded Mines

Comprehensive Assessment as of January 2026

Project Overview

Springhill, Nova Scotia, hosts one of the world's earliest and most enduring examples of mine-water geothermal utilization, operational since the late 1980s. The system exploits flooded abandoned coal mines, with an estimated 4 million cubic meters of water warmed by natural geothermal gradients to approximately 18–20°C (some deeper accesses potentially reaching 26°C).

This low-temperature resource primarily supports direct heating and cooling via water-source heat pumps for industrial parks, commercial buildings, community facilities, and educational sites. No electricity generation component exists; ongoing studies focus on expanding heating applications.

Current System Overview

  • Resource Characteristics: Water circulates convectively in the vast underground network. Proven temperatures are ultra-low-grade (18–20°C), insufficient for conventional steam cycles.
  • Technology Employed: Open-loop heat extraction with heat pumps. Water is pumped to surface heat exchangers, transfers heat, and is reinjected.
  • Scale and Impact: Serves dozens of connections; pioneering but small-scale. Challenges include mineral fouling, oxidation, and corrosion.
  • Status (2026): Heating-focused. Provincial funding supports optimization studies, but no electricity projects active.

Feasibility of Electricity Generation via Organic Rankine Cycle (ORC)

Explored hybrid systems cascade mine water through heat pumps for heating first, then binary ORC for residual power. Enhancements like phased evaporators and distributed sites were considered.

Assessment: At 18–20°C, net ORC efficiency ~4–7%. Realistic output: 50–300 kW per cluster; sub-500 kW total. No global precedents below 30–40°C for power.

Challenges and Risks

  • Poor efficiency and economics at ultra-low temperatures.
  • Resource sustainability concerns (reservoir cooling).
  • Technical issues: scaling, corrosion.

Conclusion and Recommendations

Springhill excels in thermal geothermal applications—a model for post-mining renewal. Electricity generation via ORC is theoretically possible but practically infeasible at proven temperatures due to low yields.

Recommendations: Prioritize deeper exploration for warmer water, expand heating networks, integrate with other renewables.
