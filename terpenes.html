Background and Context

Cannabis terpenes are aromatic compounds found in the trichomes of the cannabis plant, contributing to its distinctive smell, taste, and potentially its therapeutic effects. Research suggests they may work synergistically with cannabinoids like THC and CBD, a phenomenon known as the entourage effect. Terpene Details

  • Myrcene: The most common terpene in cannabis, known for sedative and pain-relieving properties. Found in strains like Mango Kush, Skunk XL, White Widow, and OG Kush.
  • Limonene: Recognized for anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Common in Sour Diesel, Super Lemon Haze, Durban Poison, and Jack Herer.
  • Linalool: Known for anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects, found in Amnesia Haze, Lavender, LA Confidential, and Pink Kush.
  • Caryophyllene: Notable for anti-inflammatory effects and aiding arthritis. Found in Girl Scout Cookies, Sour Diesel, Super Silver Haze, and Zkittles.
  • Alpha-pinene and Beta-pinene: Both offer anti-inflammatory and bronchodilator effects, common in Jack Herer, Blue Dream, Dutch Treat, and Haze Berry or OG Kush.
  • Alpha-bisabolol: Enhances absorption and provides analgesic effects, found in Harle-Tsu, Pink Kush, OG Shark, and ACDC.
  • Eucalyptol: Relieves pain and has anti-bacterial properties, present in Super Lemon Haze, Girl Scout Cookies, Headband, and AC/DC.
  • Trans-nerolidol: Offers antiparasitic and antioxidant properties, found in Sweet Skunk, Island Sweet Skunk, Skywalker OG, and Blue Dream.
