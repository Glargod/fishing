Background and Context

Cannabis terpenes are aromatic compounds found in the trichomes of the cannabis plant, contributing to its distinctive smell, taste, and potentially its therapeutic effects. Research suggests they may work synergistically with cannabinoids like THC and CBD, a phenomenon known as the entourage effect. Terpene Details

  • Myrcene: The most common terpene in cannabis, known for sedative and anti-inflammatory properties. Found in strains like Mango Kush, Skunk XL, Grand Daddy Purple, and OG Kush, it’s noted for pain relief and inflammation reduction.
  • Limonene: Recognized for mood-enhancing and stress-relieving effects, with anti-inflammatory benefits. Common in Sour Diesel and Super Lemon Haze, it’s associated with stress reduction.
  • Linalool: Known for calming and anti-anxiety effects, found in Amnesia Haze and Lavender. It’s valued for its anxiolytic and anti-inflammatory properties.
  • Caryophyllene: Notable for anti-inflammatory effects and aiding arthritis or gastrointestinal issues. Found in Girl Scout Cookies, Sour Diesel, Zkittles, and Bubba Kush.
  • Alpha-pinene and Beta-pinene: Both offer anti-inflammatory and bronchodilator effects, common in Jack Herer and Blue Dream, aiding respiratory health.
  • Alpha-bisabolol: Enhances absorption and provides analgesic effects, found in Harle-Tsu and Pink Kush.
  • Eucalyptol: Relieves pain and reduces inflammation, present in Super Lemon Haze and Girl Scout Cookies.
  • Trans-nerolidol: Offers antiparasitic and antioxidant properties, found in Sweet Skunk and Island Sweet Skunk.
  • Humulene: Known for anti-proliferative effects and appetite suppression, common in Sour Diesel and Girl Scout Cookies.
  • Delta 3 Carene: Aids bone healing and cognitive function, found in Arjan’s Ultra Haze and Bubba Kush.
  • Camphene: Acts as an antioxidant and lowers cholesterol, present in Ghost OG and Strawberry Banana.
  • Borneol: Serves as an insect repellent and has anti-cancer properties, found in Kali Mist and Hindu Kush.
  • Terpineol: Offers anti-depressant and neuroprotective effects, common in Jack Herer and White Widow.
  • Valencene: Provides anti-inflammatory and anti-allergic benefits, found in Tangie and Agent Orange.

Limitations and Future Research

Terpene effects are not universal and depend on individual physiology, dosage, and consumption method. The entourage effect is still under study, and more research is needed to confirm benefits. Strain potency varies, so consulting healthcare professionals is advised for medical use.
